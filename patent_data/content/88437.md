# Recording tape cartridge.

## Abstract
The tape cartridge comprises a case 1 , a lid member 10 having a front plate 11 and rotatably mounted on the car tridge case so be rotated between a closed position and an opened position, an opening 31 defined in the bottom portion of the front plate of the lid member for receiving a standard pin of a recording device and butting means 32, 33 projecting from the front end of the bottom wall of the cartridge case to protrude in the opening of the lid member up to a position sub stantially corresponding to the middle of thickness of the front plate when the lid member is in the closed position.

## Claims

A recording tape cartridge which comprises a cartridge case having a front wall, side walls and top and bottom walls for defining a chamber for accommodating a recording tape wound around tape reel means a lid member having a front plate and rotatably mounted on the cartridge case to be rotated between a closed position and an opened position an opening defined in the bottom portion of the front plate of the lid member for receiving a standard member of a recording device for locating the cartridge case in the recording device and butting means projecting from the front end of the bottom wall to protrude in the opening of the lid member up to a position substantially corresponding to the middle of thickness of the f ont plate when the lid member is in the closed position, said butting means having a generally flat surface in its front end.

## Description
RECORDING TAPE CARTRIDGE Field of the Invention The present invention relates to a recording tape cartridge which has a lid member pivotally connected to its front surface for covering and protecting a recording tape accommodated therein. Background of the Invention In a conventional video tape cartridge of the aforementioned type which is mounted in a recording reproducing apparatus, a recess is formed in the lower end portion of the front surface of a front lid member so that a member provided in the recording reproducing apparatus for restricting insertion of the tape cartridge is brought into contact with a part of a cartridge case exposed through the recess when the tape cartridge is mounted in the record ing reproducing apparatus. However, as the tape cartridge is repeatedly inserted in and detached from the recording reproducing apparatus, the part of the cartridge case exposed through the recess tends to be abraded or scratched by being in contact with the insertion restricting member, leading to unsightly abrasion or scratches on the exposed part.Further, dust or dirt are apt to enter the interior of the front lid member through the recess to be sticked to the recording tape. Summary of the Invention An essential object of the present invention is to provide a recording tape cartridge which effectively prevents entering of dust and or dirt inside the cartridge case in a simple construction thereby assuring excellent recording reproducing characteristics of a recording tape. Another object of the present invention is to provide a recording tape cartridge in which abrasion and or scratches on the front surface thereof are inconspicuous. A front lid member 10 is pivotally connected to the cartridge case 1 in a manner capable of being opened and closed, so as to cover the front surface of the cartridge case 1 and protect the magnetic recording tape 2 exposed along said front surface. The front lid member 10 is formed by plastic resin material, and comprises a front plate 11 for covering the entire front surface of the cartridge case 1 along the longitudinal direction, a pair of connecting members 12a and 12b rearwardly projecting from both ends of the front plate 11 and a pair of pivotal shafts 13a and 13b inwardly projecting from the connecting members 12a and 12b to be opposed to each other.The connecting members 12a and 12b are engaged with stepped wall members 15c and 15d defined in frontxportions of a pair of side walls 15a and 15b in a manner stepped inwardly of the side walls 15a and 15b while the pivotal shafts 13a and 13b are rotatably engaged in throughholes 16 defined through the stepped wall members 15c and 15d. By virtue of this construction, the front lid member 10 is pivotally rotatable about the pivotal shafts 13a and 13b between a closed position for covering the front surface of the cartridge case 1 and an upwardly opened position.Further, as hereinafter described in detail, the front lid member 10 is urged by a torsion spring 17 Fig. 5 to be in the closed position. Thus, when the video tape cartridge is not used, the front lid member 10 is in the closed position to cover and protect the outer front surface of the magnetic recording tape 2. On the other hand, when the video tape cartridge is mounted in the video tape recorder, the front lid member 10 is so opened that a tape loading pin not shown of the video tape recorder is inserted in a pocket member 19 recessedly formed in the front surface of the cartridge case 1 to draw out the magnetic recording tape 2 frontwardly from the cartridge case 1.
