# Apparatus for use in a shower unit.

## Abstract
Apparatus for use in a shower unit comprises a cylindrical brush 3 arranged to be mounted in, for example a shower cubicle 1 on a rotatable spindle 4 . A motor 5 is provided to rotate the spindle, and therefore the brush. The brush 3 is mounted, in use, beneath a shower head 2 and body shampoo is supplied to the shower head 2 so that, in use, the rotating brush washes the body of a person standing against the brush.

## Description
APPARATUS FOR USE IN A SHOWER UNIT THIS INVENTION relates to apparatus for use In a shower unit. According to the present invention, there is provided apparatus for use in a shower unit, comprising a rotatable brush, means for mounting the rotatoble brush In a shower unit and means for rotating the brush. It should be understood that, as used herein, the term shower unit includes any form of self stonding shower cubicle, or a shower fitted over a bath, for example. For a better understanding of the present invention, and to show how the same may be put into effect, reference will now be made, by way of example, to the accomponying drawings, in which FIGURE I is a perspective view from the front and one side of a shower unit incorporating apparatus in accordance with the invention and showing the use of the apparatus and FIGURE 2 is a perspective view from the back and the other side of the shower unit of Figure 1. Referring now to the drawings, there is shown a shower unit, in particular a self standing shower cubicle I incorporating apparatus in accordance with the invention. The shower cubicle includes a conventional shower head 2 controllable by conventional taps not shown . An elongate cylindrical brush 3 is secured to a spindle 4 rotatably mounted in the cubicle 1. The spindle 4 is rotated by means of a motor 5 which may be either an electric motor or a turbine propelled by mains water pressure. Where the motor is an electric motor, it is preferably mounted outside the cubicle 1. An appropriate inlet, for example a tap, allows body shampoo or soap to be supplied to the shower heod 2. The shower head 2 Is arranged to spray water containing the body shampoo onto the rotating brush 3 so that, when a person stands against the brush 3, the brush will rotate against, and thereby wash, their skin. Of course, the body shampoo or soap could be applied directly to the brush 3. The arrangement is particularly useful for people who have difficulty in moving, for example people suffering from rheumatism or arthritis.
