# Spur gear and hand tool in which it is used.

## Abstract
A spur gear 44,54 , according to one aspect of the inven tion, is formed on an axially elongated rotatable member 32 with gear teeth 44 which extend axially and radially and are formed each with an extension 48 which affords support for and tapers from the corresponding tooth 44 towards the axis of the rotatable member 32 and the root thickness of which tapers away from the corresponding tooth 44 towards a point. In another aspect, the invention provides a hand tool preferably employing the spur gear 44,54 defined as a tool drive means and having an elongated hollow enclosure com prising two complementary half shell parts 12 connected at a common end by a hinge 80 and having respective register ing openings at the other end for rotatably supporting a tool having a shaft 32 and tool drive means 44 fixed thereto, locking means being provided to hold the shell parts 12 together with the tool shaft 32 in the openings and the tool drive 44 means therebetween, the half shells 12 being releasable by said locking means so that they can be opened around the hinge 80 like a beak, providing space to remove one tool and substitute another.
