# A loading mechanism for a cassette tape recorder.

## Abstract
A loading mechanism for a cassette video recorder has for being relatively simple and compact a loading ring 12 provided with a pinch roller 4 for drawing a tape 24 out of a cassette 21 into a loading position for recording or playback and includes a gear train 19 driven by a motor 18 to rotate the ring 12 selectively in a direction for loading or unloading the tape. The gear train 19 has a reference gear 36 connected to the motor 18 , and a main drive gear 39 which drives the ring 12 through a further gear train. The reference gear 36 is selectively connected to the main gear 39 through a gear 37 for forward drive or gears 38 and 40 for reverse drive. The forward gear 37 is permanently meshed with the main gear 39 and is mounted on a plate 43 pivotable about the axis of the main gear 39 to be moved into and out of mesh with the reference gear 36 . The reverse gears 38 and 40 are also mounted on a pivotable plate 47 and are permanently meshed together, the second gear 40 being permanently meshed with the main gear 39 . The second reverse plate 47 is pivotable about the axis of the main gear 39 to move the first reverse gear 38 selectively into and out of mesh with the reference gear 36 for reverse drive.

## Description
A loading mechanism for a cassette tane recorder The present invention relates to a loading mechanism for a cassette recorder, particularly but not exclusively, a portable video cassette tape recorder. The invention seeks to provide a loading mechanism for drawing a tape out of a cassette.into a loaded position, where the tape is operatively connected to a rotary head and electrical control elements such as erase heads, for a recording or playback function and also to provide such a mechanism which is relatively simple and compact. According to the invention there is provided a loading mechanism for a cassette tape recorder having a loading ring provided with a pinch roller or draw out pin for drawing a tape out of a cassette and said tape into condition for a recording or playback operation, the mechanism including a transmitting gear train drivable by a drive mOtor to rotate said ring selectively in a direction for loading or unloading the tape control means for switching said gear train to determine the direction of rotation thereof, the gear train including a normal rotation gear and a reverse rotation gear selectively engageable with a reference gear drivably connected to the drive motor, the normal rotation and reverse rotation gears being rotatably mounted on respective pivotable plates pivotable about the axis of rotation of a main drive gear adapted to drive the loading ring and anth which the normal rotation gear is meshed directly and the reverse rotation gear through on intermediate gear to provide reversed rotation the control means being operable to pivot one of said pivotable plates to bring the gear thereon into mesh with the reference gear to thereby rotate the loading ring. Said pivotable plates may both be biassed by resilient means towards meshing the normal rotation gear and reverse rotation gear with the reference gear the control means including a slidable control lever having cam surfaces normally holding the two gears out of mesh with the reference gear during a recording or playback operation. In this case, when said control means is operated to initiate a recording or playback operation said slidable lever is movable so that one of further cam surfaces thereon pivots one of said pivotable plates to mesh the normal rotation gear thereon with said reference gear to thereby rotate the ring in a tape loading direction, a further cam of. said further cam surfaces, when playback or recording is terminated, pivoting the other of said pivotable plates to connect said reverse rotation gear with said motor to thereby rotate the ring in the unloading direction. Preferably, the mechanism includes a stop mechanism, the stop mechanism having a recess formed in the periphery of said loading ring and a rocking lever carrying a locating device adapted to locate in said recess when the ring is in the loaded position to thereby lock the ring in the loaded position. Alternatively, the gear train may be switched by a pin on the loading ring.
