# A document scanner and method for operating a document scanner.

## Abstract
A raster scanner and method for operating a raster scanner in which there is provided a calibration strip 900 for scanning by the scanner arrays 40, 41 . The calibrating signals are compared to a reference by a comparator 308 to produce a corrective signal which is stored in memory 706 . Multiple processing modes are provided with the corrective signals used in all modes to adjust the video signal output level. A separate processing channel is provided for each array with a switch 350 to switch the channels into a single output. The scan carriage 32 acceleration deacceleration is controlled through a frequency generator 790 programmed in response to detected movement of the scan carriage.

## Description
A RASTER SCANNER AND METHOD FOR OPERATING A RASTER SCANNER The invention relates to a raster scanner, and method for operating a raster scanner, and particularly to calibrating and processing video image signals produced by the scanner. In raster scanners of the type employing arrays, as for exampleCharge Coupled Devices CCDs , for scanning an original document, the signal output level of each array may, due to manufacturing tolerances, illumination variations, differences in exposure rates, etc., vary. Such variation in the signal output levels of the arrays results in a corresponding variation in the image signal levels generated by the arrays and may, as for example in the case where the image signals produced by the arrays are thresholded to provide a binary signal output, introduce distortion into the image copy produced. Further, interfacing the signal outputs of the individual arrays to form a composite signal output representative of the image line scanned may be affected. As will be understood, accurate processing and handling of the array signal outputs, including matching the output levels of the arrays with minimum noise and distortion, is necessary if a true reproduction of the image scanned is to be obtained. On the other hand, the economic feasibility of a scanner requires judicious selection of components and enlighted circuit design if an economically viable product is to result. Additionally, accurate control over the operating speed of the scan carriage, particularly during the scanning cycles essential if proper operation of the scanner is to be achieved. Failure to obtain exact scan carriage speeds may result in loss or distortion of image data as the document being processed is scanned and may upset critical internal timing constraints and synchronization of the various scanner operating components. The invention as claimed is intended to remedyethe above by providing a calibration strip having an image of preset optical density for scanning by the arrays.
